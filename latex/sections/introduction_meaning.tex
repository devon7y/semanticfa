\subsection{Meaning as Use, Distributional Semantics, and Embeddings}

A theory of word meaning is the natural starting point for analyzing a scale's
language. \citet[\S43]{wittgenstein-1953} holds, for ``a large class of cases,''
that ``the meaning of a word is its use in the language''; and a category like
\emph{games} shares no single essence, only ``a complicated network of
similarities''---\emph{family resemblance} \citep[\S\S66--67]{wittgenstein-1953}. A
construct, likewise, can cohere through graded, overlapping resemblances rather than
a hidden shared property---the relational picture distributional similarity makes
measurable.

Meaning-as-use supplies no method; \emph{distributional} theory narrows it to a
claim about co-occurrence. \citet{joos-1950} defined a unit's meaning as the
conditional probabilities of its occurrence with all others, and
\citet{harris-1954} the distribution of an element as the sum of its environments---
``difference of meaning correlates with difference of distribution.'' Modern
embeddings make this computable: \citet{mikolov-etal-2013} learn word vectors whose
offsets encode relations as arithmetic
($\mathrm{king}-\mathrm{man}+\mathrm{woman}\approx\mathrm{queen}$), and
\citet{pennington-etal-2014} factorize global word--word co-occurrence directly.
Such spaces implement a theory of semantic memory, predicting human categorization,
synonymy, similarity, and priming at near-interrater levels, with interpretable
structure recoverable post hoc \citep{gunther-etal-2019}; a complementary
prediction-based line matched human norms and released precomputed spaces behind a
query interface \citep{mandera-etal-2017}.

Scale items are sentences. The same intuition was scaled by the \emph{transformer}
\citep{vaswani-etal-2017}, whose self-attention makes each token a function of its
context, and by BERT's deep bidirectional \emph{contextual} embeddings
\citep{devlin-etal-2019}. Because averaged BERT tokens compare worse than averaged
static vectors, \citet{reimers-gurevych-2019} fine-tune a siamese Sentence-BERT
placing similar sentences close, relatedness read off as cosine similarity. This
capability---whole items in a metric space where proximity tracks meaning, alongside
validated lexical co-occurrence spaces \citep{westbury-preprint}---is what SFA uses.
