KMO was \valKmo, RMSR \valRmsr, TEFI $\valTefiMain$, and CAF \valCaf; substantial factor
correlations ($\phi = \valPhiLo$--$\valPhiHi$) again reflect the similarities'
shared positive component. \texttt{summary(fit)} adds the per-factor
McDonald's omegas:

The solution recovers the Big Five: Neuroticism anchors \valMrN,
Conscientiousness \valMrC, Openness \valMrO; \valMrA{} and \valMrE{} split Agreeableness and
Extraversion items into warmth/other-orientation and gregariousness
factors (Figure~\ref{fig:loadings};
Table~\ref{tab:s-semantic-loadings}). Communalities ranged from \valCommMin
(\valCommMinItem) to \valCommMax{} (\valCommMaxItem); the assigned--theoretical omega gaps---\valMrA{} \valOmegaAsgnA
vs.\ \valOmegaTheoA, \valMrE{} \valOmegaAsgnE{} vs.\ \valOmegaTheoE---quantify this mixing: internally
coherent factors that cross the theoretical boundary.

Conventional thresholds need not transfer to embedding-derived matrices
\citep{pokropek-2026}, so \texttt{calibrate~=~TRUE} draws Monte~Carlo reference distributions for
RMSR, CAF, and TEFI from random-unit-vector null embeddings matched on item
count and embedding dimension (100 replicates,
\valTimeCal~s):

{\scriptsize
\begin{verbatim}
> fit_cal <- sfa(big5_df, embeddings = E8, encoding = "mean_centered_pearson",
+                nfactors = 5, calibrate = TRUE, calibrate_iter = 100)
> cat("calibrate=TRUE elapsed:", round(t_cal["elapsed"], 1), "s\n")
calibrate=TRUE elapsed: 59.5 s
> fit_cal$calibration
[lists of 100 null RMSR, CAF, and TEFI values; omitted]
\end{verbatim}
}

Across the 100 nulls, RMSR ranged from \valCalRmsrMin{} to \valCalRmsrMax, CAF from \valCalCafMin{} to
\valCalCafMax, and TEFI from $\valCalTefiMin$ to $\valCalTefiMax$; the observed TEFI ($\valTefiMain$)
undercut every null---a more entropically organized five-factor
partition---while the observed RMSR (\valRmsr) and CAF (\valCaf) fell above and
below their ranges: the real similarities carry residual structure beyond
five factors that nulls lack, an index-by-index recalibration that raw
thresholds would obscure. \texttt{as\_psych(fit)} lets
\texttt{psych::fa.sort()} and \texttt{psych::factor.congruence()} treat
the fit like a response-based solution.
