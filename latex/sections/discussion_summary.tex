\subsection{Summary of Findings}

This article introduced \texttt{semanticfa}, an R package that runs the
full factor-analytic workflow (similarity construction,
retention, extraction and diagnostics, interpretation, and refinement) on
scale items' semantic structure, response-free at every stage. The
demonstration ran the full five-stage workflow on the 50 IPIP Big-Five Factor
Markers and validated results against a conventional factor analysis of
$N = \valNHuman$ respondents, yielding three findings. First, the
instrument's semantic structure is real and largely as intended: a
multi-criterion consensus (parallel analysis \valPaK, Kaiser \valKaiserK, empirical Kaiser \valEkcK, TEFI \valTefiK, EGA \valEgaK)
retained \valConsensusKWord{} factors that recovered the Big Five domains, and an
independent embedding-depth optimization agreed. Second,
semantic--behavioral coherence is graded by domain: matched Tucker
congruence with the human solution was \valPhiO{} (Openness), \valPhiC
(Conscientiousness), \valPhiN{} (Neuroticism), \valPhiE{} (Extraversion), and \valPhiA
(Agreeableness), and item-pair similarities correlated $r = \valPairR$ with
raw-response correlations over all \valNPairs{} pairs (useful convergence,
informative divergence). Third, encoding item meaning is a substantive
modeling decision: sign-flipped reverse-keyed embeddings became anti-topic
vectors aligning \emph{worst} among cosines with keyed human correlations ($r = \valArRKeyed$
vs.\ \valAtRKeyed{} unflipped), entailment- and cosine-based encodings had
mirror-image strengths (Table~\ref{tab:encodings}), and Monte Carlo
calibration showed conventional fit-index cutoffs do not transfer to
embedding-derived matrices. Refinement proved most practical: \valUvaPairsWord
semantically redundant item pairs, a response-free 25-item short form
that \emph{exceeded} the full form in recovering the theoretical partition
(NMI \valSfNmiFull{} to \valSfNmiRed, ARI \valSfAriFull{} to \valSfAriRed), though parallel analysis on the
reduced form retained \valSfKRedWord{} factors rather than \valSfKFullWord, and candidate-item
vetting that caught all three planted flaws.
