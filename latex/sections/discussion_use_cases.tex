\subsection{Applications}

The package's natural home is the stage \citet{clark-watson-2019} place
first and weight most heavily (pre-data item-pool refinement) and its
clinical analogues: screening large candidate pools, flagging redundancy
early, reaching populations and settings where large validation samples
are impractical \citep{kambeitz-etal-2025}. The demonstration models
that pass: redundancy screening exposed \valUvaPairsWord{}
near-paraphrase pairs (A24~$\sim$~A29 leading, wTO \valWtoTop), repetition
inflating internal consistency and wording-driven correlations, not
content \citep{christensen-etal-2023,kojima-etal-2026}. Vetting correctly
typed three new items as cross-loader (gap \valIfGap), near-duplicate (cosine
\valIfNearest), and weak match (\valIfWeakSim{} vs.\ domain average \valIfWeakAvg). Response-free
shortening halved the instrument yet \emph{improved} theoretical-partition
recovery (NMI \valSfNmiFull{} to \valSfNmiRed{} and ARI \valSfAriFull{} to \valSfAriRed, with parallel-analysis
retention dropping to \valSfKRedWord{} factors). Peripheral, not central,
items had blurred construct boundaries
\citep{huang-etal-2025, kojima-etal-2026}. The attenuation paradox
(\citealp{clark-watson-2019}) transfers: maximizing semantic homogeneity,
like internal consistency, can prune breadth the construct requires, so
an anchor-centrality-optimized short form still owes response-based
validation, a caveat the package itself prints
\citep{jung-seo-2025,wang-etal-2026}.

Second, response data may be scarce, absent, or compromised:
\citet{muller-2026} recovered semantic structure at $n = 55$ after the
same instrument's confirmatory factor analysis collapsed, and
\texttt{semanticfa}, needing no responses, lets structural screening
precede recruitment. The synthetic respondents of
\citet{westwood-2025}, passing 99.8\% of attention checks and biasing
results toward inferred hypotheses, argue for analysis that touches no
response pool and against model-derived structure passing as human
evidence. Hence the congruence machinery: semantic--behavioral coherence
is demonstrated, never presumed. No current language model reproduces
the human ability distribution \citep{liu-etal-2025}, and
virtual-respondent frameworks disclaim replicating human psychological
processes \citep{lim-etal-2025}.

Third, the tools scale across instruments: the jingle--jangle screen,
rightly silent on the deliberately well-constructed Big-Five Factor
Markers, targets cross-instrument screening, where label--content
misalignment is endemic \citep{wulff-mata-2025,wulff-mata-2026}, and
\texttt{semanticfa}'s evaluation-time auditing of existing instruments
complements AIGENIE's generation-time vetting of new item pools
\citep{russell-lasalandra-etal-2026}. Finally, the design choices are
methodological commitments: an explicit, programmatic embedding pipeline
recovers structure that prompted chatbots do not \citep{huang-etal-2025}.
Open, locally run models make sensitive-item analysis transparent and
privacy-respecting \citep{hussain-etal-2024,low-etal-preprint}. Pinned
model identity freezes the measurement instrument across reanalyses, and
the pure-R, offline factor-analytic core (only the NLI matrix and
live-embedding demonstrations need Python or the network) runs
identically in a data-sovereignty-constrained clinic and a teaching
laboratory. We commend the transcript-anchored reporting used
here (nearly every number checkable against a captured console
transcript, the jackknife intervals excepted) to software papers.
