\subsection{The \texttt{semanticfa} Pipeline}

The package \citep{yanitski-westbury-2026} exposes five stages as documented R functions \citep{rcoreteam-2025}.

\textbf{Similarity.} \texttt{sfa\_similarity()} turns embeddings into an item-by-item similarity matrix under four encodings: \texttt{atomic} (cosines of L2-normalized vectors) and \texttt{atomic\_reversed} (reverse-keyed embeddings times $-1$), the pseudo-factor encodings of \citet{guenole-etal-2025}, plus two keying-free encodings: \texttt{squid}, which subtracts the questionnaire-mean embedding to recover the negative relations raw cosines suppress, notably for reverse-keyed items \citep{pellert-etal-2026}, and \texttt{mean\_centered\_pearson}, which centers each vector across its dimensions so that cosine equals Pearson correlation, yielding a genuine correlation matrix that can in principle depart from the raw cosines \citep{pokropek-2026}. A fifth, embedding-free route, \texttt{sfa\_nli\_matrix()}, scores pairs with the NLI cross-encoder above \citep{bowman-etal-2015} as entailment minus contradiction probability, the signed mapping of \citet{hommel-arslan-2025}, and projects indefinite results (signed matrices need not be positive semi-definite), with notice, to a nearby positive-definite correlation matrix (eigenvalues floored, diagonal rescaled to one).

\textbf{Retention.} \texttt{sfa\_parallel()} adapts parallel analysis \citep{horn-1965} to embeddings---null eigenvalues come from similarity matrices of random unit vectors in the embedding dimension, no sample size involved---retaining by default factors above the 95th percentile of 100 null replicates. \texttt{sfa\_nfactors()} reports the consensus of up to five criteria: parallel analysis; the latent-root-one rule (eigenvalues above one; \citealp{kaiser-1960}); the empirical Kaiser criterion (\texttt{sfa\_ekc()}), whose reference eigenvalues start at the null-model maximum and apply a serial correction for the variance preceding factors have already absorbed, with the embedding dimension in the sample-size role \citep{braeken-vanassen-2017}; minimum TEFI \citep{golino-2026}; and EGA (triangulated maximally filtered graph (TMFG) networks with Walktrap detection, via EGAnet; \citealp{golino-christensen-2026}), the framework that best recovered embedding dimensionality for \citet{garrido-etal-2025}, whose validation used EBICglasso networks (TMFG stands in here because it needs no sample size). Velicer's minimum average partial is also available (\texttt{sfa\_map()}; \citealp{velicer-1976}) but is not a default vote, because on embedding similarity matrices it tracks reliably estimated minor structure well past the interpretable factor count. A separate diagnostic, \texttt{sfa\_cd()}, adapts the comparison-data method of \citet{ruscio-roche-2012} and reports a misfit profile over candidate factor counts rather than a vote: it shows whether a matrix carries a crisp factor boundary or graded structure (see Supplementary Materials). \texttt{sfa\_dimselect()} adapts the embedding-depth optimization of \citet{golino-2026}, choosing the nested leading-coordinate depth maximizing $0.70 \times \mathrm{NMI} - 0.30 \times \mathrm{TEFI}_{\mathrm{norm}}$.

\textbf{Extraction and diagnostics.} \texttt{sfa()} runs the pipeline end to end from raw text, embeddings, or a similarity matrix, with minimum-residual factoring and oblimin rotation (oblique, in the varimax tradition; \citealp{kaiser-1958}) via psych \citep{revelle-2026}. Fits report Kaiser--Meyer--Olkin (KMO) sampling adequacy, partition-based TEFI \citep{golino-2026}, root mean square residual (RMSR), common part accounted for (CAF), McDonald's omega per factor (empirical and best-matching theoretical sets), and, given domain labels, a factor-by-construct dominant average absolute loading (DAAL) matrix \citep{guenole-etal-2025}. On the chance-baseline logic above, \texttt{calibrate = TRUE} re-derives RMSR, CAF, and TEFI reference distributions from random-unit-vector null replicates with no shared semantic structure, adapting to a structure-free null the corpus-resampling reference distributions of \citet{pokropek-2026}, whose null words retain their corpus's baseline thematic similarity; \texttt{as\_psych()} converts fits to \texttt{psych::fa} objects.

\textbf{Interpretation.} Following \citet{wulff-mata-2025}, \texttt{sfa\_anchor()} tabulates ``belonging,'' a semantic loadings analogue, as cosine similarity to construct anchors (assigned-item centroids or label embeddings), flagging items weakest on their own construct or closer to a rival. \texttt{sfa\_project()} applies the semantic projection of \citet{grand-etal-2022} introduced above, scores rescaled so that 0 and 1 denote the two poles. \texttt{sfa\_congruence()} compares a fit with a theoretical partition, human EFA, or correlation matrix on up to five metrics: factorwise Tucker's $\phi$ (\texttt{psych::factor.congruence}; \citealp{revelle-2026}); normalized mutual information (NMI) between partitions \citep{golino-2026,garrido-etal-2025}; the adjusted Rand index (ARI) \citep{hubert-arabie-1985}; a normalized Frobenius matrix similarity; and a disattenuated item-pair correlation (over split-half reliabilities, missing when either is negative). \texttt{sfa\_jinglejangle()} screens scale sets for jingle--jangle fallacies---label versus item-content similarity per scale pair \citep{wulff-mata-2025,wulff-mata-2026}---flagging by default at $|\,\mathrm{content} - \mathrm{label}\,| \geq 0.20$.

\textbf{Refinement.} \texttt{sfa\_redundancy()} flags locally dependent pairs by faithful UVA \citep{christensen-etal-2023}---absolute weighted topological overlap on an EBICglasso network of the similarity matrix \citep{golino-christensen-2026}, recommended cutoff 0.25---suggesting removals that keep one item per cluster; a direct cosine criterion is a simpler alternative. \texttt{sfa\_simplify()} builds response-free short forms from the items most representative of each construct's anchor or medoid \citep{jung-seo-2025,wang-etal-2026}, reporting retention and theory-recovery statistics against the original---candidates pending psychometric validation. \texttt{sfa\_item\_fit()} extends the anchoring logic of \citet{wulff-mata-2025} to vet newly written items against an existing fit: each is embedded with a named model and scored per construct on similarity to the construct name and its items, cross-loading gap to the second-best construct, and near-duplicates above a redundancy cutoff. \texttt{sfa\_corplot()} draws the similarity matrix as a factor-grouped heatmap; \texttt{sfa\_itemplot()} maps items to two dimensions by t-SNE \citep{vandermaaten-hinton-2008}, UMAP, principal components, or classical multidimensional scaling; and \texttt{plot()} methods add a scree plot with parallel-analysis overlay and a loadings heatmap.
