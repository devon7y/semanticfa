\subsection{Semantic--Behavioral Coherence}

Cleaning the human benchmark (see Method) left \valNHuman{} of
\valNHumanRaw{} respondents, the \valNRev{} reverse-keyed items rescored
($6 - x$) for keyed analyses:

{\scriptsize
\begin{verbatim}
> keep <- rowSums(human_raw < 1 | human_raw > 5 | is.na(human_raw)) == 0
> human <- human_raw[keep, ]
> cat("Respondents after cleaning:", n_human, "of", nrow(human_raw), "\n")
Respondents after cleaning: 874434 of 1015341
> human_fa <- psych::fa(R_human, nfactors = 5, n.obs = n_human,
+                       rotate = "oblimin", fm = "minres")
\end{verbatim}
}

Five factors were fixed a priori \citep{goldberg-1990}. Parallel
analysis suggested \valHumanPaKWord{} (at $N = \valNHuman$ even trivial
factors clear noise), but five reproduced the Big Five with simple
structure (Table~\ref{tab:s-human-loadings}).

\subsubsection{Factor-Level Congruence}

\texttt{sfa\_congruence()} takes a fitted \texttt{psych} model as the
target:

{\scriptsize
\begin{verbatim}

Each semantic factor found a distinct human counterpart
(Table~\ref{tab:tucker}): Openness, Conscientiousness, and Neuroticism
strongly ($\phi = \valPhiO$, \valPhiC, \valPhiN), and Extraversion (\valPhiE) and Agreeableness
(\valPhiA) weakly, the former matching human Agreeableness nearly as well
(\valPhiEA). Overall, $\bar{\phi} = \valPhiMean$, 95\% CI
$[\valPhiMeanLo, \valPhiMeanHi]$ (item-level jackknife, see Method),
NMI = \valNmiTheory, ARI = \valAriTheory, and
normalized Frobenius similarity \valFrob between the correlation
structures.

Three patterns stand out. First, \texttt{atomic\_reversed}, an
anti-topic vector, not reverse-scoring's analogue (see
Discussion), aligned worst among cosines with keyed correlations
($r_{\text{keyed}} = \valArRKeyed$ vs.\ \valAtRKeyed{} unflipped) and ordinarily only
with raw ($r_{\text{raw}} = \valArRRaw$).

Second, NLI mirrors the cosines: best on the theoretical partition
(NMI = \valNliNmi, ARI = \valNliAri) and on raw pairwise correlations
($r_{\text{raw}} = \valNliRRaw$), worst on loading shape
($\bar{\phi} = \valNliPhi$), cosine the reverse ($\bar{\phi} = \valPhiMean$,
$r_{\text{raw}} = \valMcpRRaw$), directional entailment versus graded
geometry (see Discussion).

Third, SQuID won keyed pair-level agreement ($r_{\text{keyed}} = \valSqRKeyed$,
$r_{\text{dis}}$ saturated at \valSqDis) yet was essentially unfactorable
(KMO = \valSqKmo), its differencing keeping pairwise relations but
stripping extractable common variance. Only keying-free
mean-centered Pearson combined near-best theory congruence (within
\valNmiGapNli{} NMI), the best loading-shape match ($\bar{\phi} = \valPhiMean$), and a
genuine, well-conditioned correlation matrix (KMO = \valKmo), hence the
main fit. On these high-dimensional vectors it is numerically
indistinguishable from \texttt{atomic} (Table~\ref{tab:encodings}), so
the demonstration's conclusions for the main encoding attach to the
cosine family as a whole.

\subsubsection{Item-Pair Agreement}

Across all 1,225 item pairs, structure from language alone matched the
raw correlations of \valNHuman{} respondents at $r = \valPairR$, 95\% CI
$[\valPairRLo, \valPairRHi]$ (Figure~\ref{fig:semantic-human}).

Semantic--behavioral coherence is thus a measured relation: strong
convergence for three domains, partial where warmth--sociability
language blurs a boundary response covariance keeps sharp, and
informative, encoding-specific divergence where the similarity model
redefines ``meaning the same thing.''
