\abstract{%
The items of a psychometric scale constitute a proposal, written in ordinary
language, about the structure of a construct, and language-model embeddings
make that proposal measurable before any respondent is recruited. We
introduce \texttt{semanticfa}, an R package for semantic factor analysis:
factor analysis of the similarity structure of language-model embeddings of
scale items, requiring no response data. The package implements the full
workflow: building an item-similarity matrix, choosing the number of
factors, fitting and diagnosing the model, interpreting the factors, and
refining the item pool (redundant items, short forms, candidate-item
vetting). A reproducible demonstration applies every stage to the 50-item
International Personality Item Pool Big-Five Factor Markers, embedding items
with Qwen3-Embedding-8B and validating the semantic structure against a
factor analysis of \valNHuman{} respondents. The workflow retained
\valConsensusKWord{} factors that recovered the Big Five domains: congruence
with the human factors ranged from \valPhiO{} for Openness to \valPhiA{} for
Agreeableness, and item pairs closer in meaning correlated more strongly
across respondents ($r = \valPairR$ across \valNPairs{} pairs). Encoding
choices mattered: sign-flipping reverse-keyed items' embeddings, the seeming
analogue of reverse-scoring, worsened agreement with scored human responses,
and a 25-item short form built from the most semantically representative
items matched the intended five domains better than the full inventory,
though parallel analysis retained \valSfKRedWord{} factors rather than
\valSfKFullWord. We frame this agreement, semantic--behavioral
coherence, as an estimable relation in a construct's nomological network: a
complement to, not a replacement for, response-based factor analysis. The
package is available on CRAN.
}
\keywords{semantic factor analysis, language models, embeddings, construct validity, scale development, R package}
