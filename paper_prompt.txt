Pillar 1 — Theory: what it means to analyze the language of a scale
Core ideas:
- Constructs live in a nomological network (Cronbach & Meehl): validity is a web of
  relations, not a hidden essence.
- A scale's items are an operationalized semantic proposal for the construct
  (Wittgenstein meaning-as-use; reflective vs. formative indicators).
- Two relational structures: behavioral (person x item covariance) vs. semantic (geometry
  of item meaning). Classical FA recovers behavioral clustering; SFA recovers
  linguistic-usage clustering — structurally parallel, different data source.
- New contribution: semantic-behavioral coherence as a relation in the nomological network.
  Convergence strengthens a construct; divergence is itself informative (compression,
  ambiguity, jingle-jangle, theoretical underspecification).
- SFA needs no respondents -> enables scale evaluation before data collection.

Pillar 2 — Review: language models and psychometrics
Synthesize the papers_txt corpus, organized by what each line of work does:

  Hommel & Arslan, Schoenegger, Ravenda, Feraco).
- Taxonomy, jingle-jangle, incommensurability (Wulff & Mata 2025/2026, Stanghellini).
- Clinical / psychopathology structure from embeddings (Kambeitz, Kojima).
- Methods & pipelines: SQuID centering (Pellert), CFA with embeddings (Pokropek),
  Unique Variable Analysis (Christensen), EGA on embeddings (Golino, Garrido),
  short forms (Jung & Seo, Wang), tutorials/overviews (Hussain, Low).
- Critiques and limits (Uher: statistics is not measurement).
Position semanticfa as the first general-purpose toolkit unifying these threads in one
reproducible R workflow.

Pillar 3 — Demonstration: the package end-to-end on the Big Five
One reproducible worked example on data(big5) that exercises every exported function,
grouped by workflow stage:
- Embed & build similarity: the main results use the bundled data(big5)$embeddings
  (Qwen3-Embedding-8B); also demonstrate sfa_embed and sfa_load_npz as features (how a user
  would generate or load their own vectors), then sfa_similarity (4 encodings:
  atomic / atomic_reversed / squid / mean_centered_pearson) and sfa_nli_matrix (signed/NLI).
- Recover structure: sfa() one-call pipeline; sfa_nfactors (parallel / Kaiser / TEFI / EGA),
  sfa_parallel (embedding-adapted), sfa_dimselect (depth); as_psych (hand off to psych).
- Interpret: sfa_anchor (semantic loadings table), sfa_project (bipolar axes),
  sfa_congruence (Tucker phi, NMI, ARI, Frobenius, disattenuated), sfa_jinglejangle.
- Refine before data: sfa_redundancy (UVA), sfa_simplify (short forms),
  sfa_item_fit (vet a new candidate item).
- Visualize: sfa_corplot, sfa_itemplot (t-SNE / UMAP / PCA / MDS), plot(fit, "scree").
- Fit diagnostics: KMO, TEFI, RMSR, CAF, McDonald's omega, DAAL; summary(fit);

- Compare to human data: readers will want the semantic structure checked against real

  semantic-behavioral coherence idea from Pillar 1 — report the convergence and discuss
  any divergence. Emphasize that SFA itself used no responses; the human data is only the
